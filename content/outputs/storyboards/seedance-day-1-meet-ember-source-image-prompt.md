## Copy/Paste Image Prompt

```text
Create a clean vertical 9:16 children's storybook promo image for a Seedance image-to-video source frame.

Show Ember, a tiny adorable reddish-orange baby dragon guide, centered and clearly visible from head to small body. Ember has soft pumpkin-orange and coral shading, a cream belly, tiny shiny golden spiral-comma horns, large glossy blue-teal eyes, a plain bright blue-teal scarf, and a tiny plain brown crossbody satchel with dull-gold button clasps. Ember has rounded plush-like baby-dragon proportions, soft storybook scale texture, bright friendly eyes, and a cheerful curious expression.

Use a simple, stable, low-detail cozy background made for AI video animation: a soft warm golden color wash, a few large blurred lantern-like light shapes far behind Ember, and gentle magical warmth. Keep the background shallow, uncluttered, and mostly still-looking. Leave clean space around Ember so the image can be animated with a slow push-in and cropped for Shorts/Reels.

Style: polished soft 2.25D children's storybook illustration, lightly illustrated edges, rounded plush-like forms, gentle sculpted volume, painterly shading, warm cheerful lighting, smooth clean surfaces, cute and non-scary.

Composition: vertical 9:16, Ember centered, full character visible, friendly eye contact, enough margin above horns and below feet, simple background with no busy details.

No text. No title. No words. No letters. No signs. No labels. No logos. No watermark. No captions. No page numbers. No crowds. No buildings. No shelves. No market stalls. No extra characters. No extra props. No hidden-object markings. Do not make Ember realistic, scary, smug, angry, fox-like, cat-like, or human-like. Do not change Ember's blue-teal scarf or brown satchel.
```

## Why This Prompt Is Seedance-Friendly

- Single centered character.
- Simple stable background.
- Few moving possibilities: blink, tiny wave, soft light shimmer, slow push-in.
- No readable text for the video model to distort.
- No crowds or clutter for the model to invent motion from.
