## Live Procedure

1. Open Seedance in Chromium.
2. Stop if Seedance asks for login, account verification, payment, subscription, CAPTCHA, or any permission prompt that was not already approved.
3. Choose image-to-video, not text-only generation.
4. Upload the approved Day 1 Meet Ember source image.
5. If reference uploads are available, add `Ember-001`, `Ember-002`, and `Ember-003`.
6. Set duration to 15 seconds if available.
7. Choose vertical output if Seedance offers it. If not, keep the cleanest/default output and crop later in CapCut.
8. Paste the Seedance prompt from the storyboard.
9. Before generating, verify:
   - correct source image is visible
   - prompt says no generated captions, logos, labels, watermarks, or extra text
   - prompt asks for a simple, stable, low-detail background
   - prompt blocks crowds, buildings, shelves, market stalls, extra characters, and new props
   - Ember remains on-model
   - no sales/available-now claim appears anywhere
10. Generate only after the draft screen passes the checks above.
11. Save a screenshot of the Seedance setup screen and the finished result page if possible.
12. Download the video output.

## Copy/Paste Seedance Prompt

```text
Animate this approved Ember promo image as a gentle 15-second image-to-video character intro for children ages 5-8. Keep the original Ember character design, colors, and cozy soft 2.25D storybook look. Use a simple, stable, low-detail background: soft warm color wash, a few blurred lantern-like light shapes, and no busy festival detail. Ember gives one tiny friendly wave, blinks once or twice, and smiles warmly toward the viewer. Add subtle warm light shimmer, two or three tiny slow sparkles, a slow push-in camera motion, and very mild parallax between Ember and the simple blurred background. Keep motion calm, readable, cute, and non-scary. Do not add crowds, buildings, shelves, market stalls, extra characters, new props, readable text, signs, labels, logos, watermarks, or generated captions. Do not change Ember's scarf or satchel, and do not make Ember realistic.
```

## QA After Download

- Ember still looks like the approved baby dragon guide.
- Scarf, satchel, horns, eyes, color, and proportions are preserved.
- Motion is gentle, warm, and kid-friendly.
- Background stays simple, stable, and low-detail.
- No extra text, labels, logos, watermarks, gibberish, or captions were generated by the model.
- No extra characters, crowd, buildings, shelves, market stalls, or new props.
- No scary lighting, frantic motion, or off-model realism.
- The source image is still recognizable.
- The clip can be cropped or edited in CapCut without cutting off Ember.
