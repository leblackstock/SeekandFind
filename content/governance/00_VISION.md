# Seek-and-Find Vision

## Purpose

This is the file to come back to when a prompt, page, image, or automation output feels uncertain.

It holds the overall vision for the seek-and-find books we create, including the Ember series. It is not a checklist, a prompt template, or a replacement for the detailed rules. The detailed rules are for execution. This file is for taste, intent, and belonging.

When something feels technically compliant but still wrong, start here.

## Overall Vision For Seek-and-Find Books

These books should feel inviting, giftable, replayable, visually rich, and fair.

Each page should make children, families, or the intended audience want to lean closer, search around, notice small stories, and come back later. The best pages feel full of discovery without turning into chaos. They have places to look, reasons to explore, and little details that feel like they belong there.

A seek-and-find page should not be just a pretty illustration with objects sprinkled on top. It should feel like a real place with a playful search built into it.

Searchable richness comes from tradeoffs, not piling on. Every page has a visual budget. When the prompt adds findable objects, it should reduce repeated decorative filler so the image stays readable.

Theme motifs can still earn their place. A flower, lantern, crystal, shell, mushroom, ribbon, or jar can double as a searchable object when it is visually distinct enough to find. Repeated generic versions of the same motif should stay limited, or they become background texture instead of gameplay.

## Universal Must-Haves

- A clear audience promise.
- Fair hidden-object play.
- Strong visual appeal at thumbnail size and full-page size.
- Intentional detail zones instead of random clutter.
- Recognizable objects suited to the target audience.
- Searchable motifs that are distinct enough to find, not just repeated decoration.
- Child-nameable finds when the book is for children.
- Print-safe composition with important details kept away from trim and gutter risk.
- Original characters, worlds, themes, and visual identity.
- Enough charm, story, and discovery to feel like a real book, not just a generated puzzle image.

## Universal Must-Not-Haves

- No copied famous seek-and-find trade dress.
- No unreadable clutter.
- No hidden objects marked with circles, arrows, labels, boxes, halos, highlights, or answer marks.
- No fake or generated text inside seek images.
- No random floor scatter as the main hiding strategy.
- No adding searchable objects by simply piling them on top of already-busy decoration.
- No repeated decorative motif taking over the page until the search objects disappear.
- No pages that are pretty but not actually searchable.
- No prompts that invent canon, page facts, mission items, character details, or source truth.

## Ember Series Vision

Ember books are warm, magical, cozy children's seek-and-find adventures for ages 5-8.

Ember is the small baby dragon guide who helps the child enter the world. He is not the hidden target. He should make the page feel friendly and alive without taking over the search.

Each spread should feel like a story moment with a real place, a mission item, and discoverable details. The world should feel cheerful, safe, magical, and easy to love. Ember's adventures should feel like something a child could return to with affection, not just solve once and forget.

## Ember Must-Haves

- Ember appears exactly once on seek pages as a guide/helper.
- Ember stays small-cat sized, reddish-orange, with a blue-teal scarf, brown satchel, golden spiral-comma horns, and glossy blue-teal eyes.
- Mission items appear once and are hidden fairly.
- Searchable objects belong naturally in the setting.
- Repeated flowers, lanterns, crystals, sparkles, or other motifs stay controlled unless specific ones are intentionally made findable.
- Scenes have depth: foreground, midground, and background.
- Each page supports its paired story/list page.
- The page feels warm, child-safe, magical, and storybook-friendly.

## Ember Must-Not-Haves

- Ember must not dominate seek pages like cover art.
- Ember must not be hidden as the mission item.
- No off-model fox, cat, human wizard, or generic fantasy-animal Ember.
- No scary, smug, angry, or overly realistic Ember.
- No invented page locations or mission items unless the user explicitly approves a canon change.
- No story/list text baked into seek images.

## How To Use This File

Before fixing a prompt or image, ask: "Does this serve the Vision?"

If the Vision and the detailed rules appear to disagree, pause and reconcile them instead of forcing an output. Do not use this file to ignore the rules, and do not use the rules to produce something that violates the heart of the book.

Use the detailed rules for execution. Use this file for taste, intent, and belonging.
